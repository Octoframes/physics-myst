Polar Coordinates¶

See radial and theta for animations. See Figure 1 for a static image, and trans and transback .

Polar coordinates are a two-dimensional coordinate system in which each point on a plane is determined by a distance from a reference point and an angle from a reference direction.

The reference point (also called the pole) is typically denoted as OOO. The ray from the pole in the reference direction is the positive x-axis, and it's often referred to as the polar axis.

The distance from the pole is called the radial coordinate or radius, and it's often denoted by rrr. The angle from the polar axis is the angular coordinate or the polar angle, typically denoted by θ\thetaθ.

In summary, a point in the plane can be expressed in polar coordinates as (r,θ)(r, \theta)(r,θ), where rrr is the radial distance and θ\thetaθ is the polar angle.

To convert from Cartesian coordinates (x,y)(x, y)(x,y) to polar coordinates (r,θ)(r, \theta)(r,θ):

[rθ]=[x2+y2atan2(y,x)]\begin{bmatrix}r \\ \theta \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} \sqrt{x^2 + y^2} \\ \text{atan2}(y, x) \end{bmatrix}[rθ​]=[x2+y2​atan2(y,x)​]
(1)

To convert from polar coordinates (r,θ)(r, \theta)(r,θ) to Cartesian coordinates (x,y)(x, y)(x,y):

[xy]=r⋅[cos⁡(θ)sin⁡(θ)]\begin{bmatrix}x \\ y \end{bmatrix} = r \cdot \begin{bmatrix} \cos(\theta) \\ \sin(\theta) \end{bmatrix}[xy​]=r⋅[cos(θ)sin(θ)​]
(2)

Note:

  1. The  \sqrt{\text{ }} ​ function returns the square root of a number.
  2. The atan2\text{atan2}atan2 function returns the angle in radians between the positive x-axis and the point given by the coordinates on the two-dimensional plane.
  3. The cos⁡\coscos and sin⁡\sinsin functions return the cosine and sine of an angle respectively.
